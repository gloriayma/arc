# scooby: What's Borrowable for sc_nona

**Paper:** Hingerl, Martens, Karollus, Manz, Buenrostro, Theis, Gagneur. *scooby: modeling multimodal genomic profiles from DNA sequence at single-cell resolution.* Nat Methods 22:2275–2285 (Nov 2025). DOI 10.1038/s41592-025-02854-5.
**Code:** github.com/gagneurlab/scooby (model), github.com/gagneurlab/scooby_reproducibility (notebooks), github.com/lauradmartens/SnapATAC2 (adapted data loader), github.com/lauradmartens/peft (LoRA fork).
**Data:** Zenodo 10.5281/zenodo.15517064 (suppl) and 10.5281/zenodo.15517072 (training data).

---

## Executive summary (3 bullets)

- **Top borrowable**: the *training-data plumbing*, not the model. scooby's adapted SnapATAC2 (split-read-aware, stores read length at start position as a sparse cell × genome matrix; Extended Data Fig. 2 and Methods "Efficient read coverage extraction") plus the on-the-fly coverage track aggregator are the single most lift-able artifact for sc_nona — they solve precisely the "go from per-cell BAM to per-cell BigWig-like tracks for training" problem that sc_nona will hit. The cell-specific decoder design (a small MLP that maps a 14-dim cell embedding to the *weights* of a 1×1 position-wise conv applied to Borzoi's penultimate embedding; Extended Data Fig. 1) is a clean, parameter-efficient pattern worth copying even if sc_nona's "axis" is donor rather than cell-state.
- **Top differentiator**: sc_nona's variation-over-genotypes axis is genuinely orthogonal. scooby is run on the *human reference* (hg38) with no per-donor genome treatment and no allele-resolved tracks — variant effect prediction is done by in-silico SNP substitution into the reference. sc_nona's Smart-seq2 + N-masked diploid + SNPsplit + hap1/hap2 tracks give it a direct empirical readout of allelic effects that scooby's eQTL benchmarks have to *infer*. The MSA-across-donors attention idea has zero analog in scooby (no cross-cell or cross-donor attention; cells are conditioned independently).
- **Biggest positioning risk**: scooby already owns the framing "single-cell-resolution sequence-to-function via cell-conditioned Borzoi head + LoRA." If sc_nona positions itself as "scooby but with Smart-seq2," reviewers will ask whether the upside of full-length coverage justifies a 100-1000× smaller cell budget (HipSci sc covers thousands of cells from ~125 donors; scooby's NeurIPS dataset is 63,683 cells from one tissue). sc_nona needs to be framed around **variation-across-donors + allele resolution + DNA-LM pretraining substrate**, not "single-cell expression prediction" — that fight is already lost on cell count.

## 1. Data preprocessing pipeline

### Pipeline from raw reads to model-ready data

scooby does *not* re-align reads. It starts from outputs of standard pipelines (page 2275 first column; Methods "Data acquisition"):

- **scRNA-seq**: starts from the **10x Cell Ranger output BAMs** (Cell Ranger v6.1.1 / v8.0.1 in different datasets), filtering reads that pass Cell Ranger's `xf:i:25` flag (Methods "Processing of RNA BAM files"). They explicitly call out *the 10x BAMs include all reads (also low-quality ones), we filtered for reads that were marked as valid* — so the filter is downstream of the aligner, not a re-alignment.
- **scATAC-seq**: starts from Cell Ranger ATAC v2.1.0 fragment files.
- **Reference**: `refdata-cellranger-arc-GRCh38-2020-A-2.0.0` (plain hg38) and `refdata-gex-GRCh38-2020-A` for the gene track. No personal genome, no N-masking, no allele-specific handling anywhere.
- **Storage**: They use an **adapted SnapATAC2 fork** (github.com/lauradmartens/SnapATAC2, rustup 1.28.1) that they modified to store *RNA* reads in the SnapATAC AnnData format. The key trick (Extended Data Fig. 2): each read becomes a single sparse-matrix entry at its start position, with the matrix value = read length (signed by strand). Split reads (RNA-only concern) are stored as multiple entries, one per contiguous fragment. ATAC reads become two Tn5 insertion sites.
- **Normalization**: they follow Borzoi's "squashed scale" approach — `softclip` to 5 for RNA (Methods "Data preparation for training": *"we used the same squashed-scale approach as Borzoi, but set clip_soft to 5, such that the fraction of soft-clipped values at the single-cell level was similar to the one of Borzoi's tracks"*). For ATAC, they additionally multiply by 0.05 to bring single-cell signal to pseudo-bulk scale. **No CPM, no log1p, no per-cell library-size normalization.**
- **Bin size**: 32 bp (inherited from Borzoi, 524,288 bp input → 6,144 bins × 32 bp output, Methods "Model").
- **Strandedness**: stranded RNA (output shape `(1921, 2, 1)` — two strands), unstranded ATAC (`(1921, 1, 1)`) — see Methods. Reverse-complement augmentation is applied during training with target re-flipping (Methods "Training procedure").
- **Coverage tracks generated on-the-fly during training** from the sparse SnapATAC matrix — no precomputed per-cell BigWigs except for evaluation, where `sp.ex.export_coverage` is used for pseudobulk BigWigs.

### Cells with low coverage / dropouts

There is no explicit dropout-aware loss term. Instead:

- **Genes/peaks present in < 1% of cells are excluded from training targets** (Methods "Generation of cell embeddings", and Sample-size section of Reporting Summary).
- Doublets are filtered with Scrublet at default parameters (Methods "Data acquisition").
- The model implicitly handles dropout by training on *single-cell-level* targets that include many zeros — the Poisson/multinomial loss inherited from Borzoi (Methods "Training procedure": *"We used the same weighting scheme for the Poisson and multinomial loss terms as in the original Borzoi implementation"*) tolerates this.
- For evaluation, they aggregate over the 100 nearest neighbors of each test cell as a smoothed pseudo-ground-truth (Fig. 1c, 1d) — explicitly because single-cell signal is too sparse to score against directly.

### Per-cell sparse counts to coverage track

Not really — they *skip the counts-matrix step entirely for training*. They go: BAM → SnapATAC sparse `(cell × position, value = read length)` → on-the-fly per-cell coverage track of shape `(strand, length)` summed inside a 32-bp bin. Gene counts are only used for *evaluation* (Methods "Gene count evaluation"), computed by summing predicted coverage over exons.

### Transferability to Smart-seq2 / per-donor genomes

**The SnapATAC2 fork is directly liftable** — the abstraction (sparse `cell × position` matrix, with strand sign and read length encoded) is agnostic to whether the BAM came from 10x or Smart-seq2, and *more* useful for Smart-seq2 because Smart-seq2 reads are full-length (no 3' bias) so the split-read handling (Extended Data Fig. 2) becomes essential rather than incidental. The split-read storage is in fact the only piece of the paper that *explicitly cares about RNA biology* (vs. ATAC), and Smart-seq2 has more split reads per cell than 10x.

What does **not** transfer cleanly:

- They start from Cell Ranger BAMs and inherit Cell Ranger's barcode handling, UMI dedup, and `xf:i:25` filter. Smart-seq2 has no UMIs and no Cell Ranger — sc_nona will need its own equivalent (STAR per-cell + samtools-based dedup, or a zUMIs-equivalent flow). The user's existing `3.1_nona_alignment_pipeline.md` should specify this.
- They have no concept of haplotype-separated tracks. sc_nona's hap1/hap2/diploid-sum tracks are an extra output dimension scooby never models. The SnapATAC matrix would need to become `(cell × haplotype × position)` or three parallel matrices — straightforward but additive.
- They have no per-donor genome lift-over. scooby's bin coordinates are hg38; sc_nona's bin coordinates would be per-donor. The user will need a g2gtools-style coordinate-system reconciliation that scooby doesn't have to face.

## 2. Architecture (Borzoi-based)

### Backbone

- **Borzoi v0.0.2 PyTorch port** (`github.com/johahi/borzoi-pytorch`), initialized from **replicate 0, test fold 3, validation fold 4** of the original TensorFlow Borzoi (Methods "Training procedure"). They strip the original human and mouse output heads and keep only conv stem → U-Net skip → U-Net transformer → upsampling stem (Extended Data Fig. 1). The output is the penultimate Borzoi sequence embedding: shape `(1920, 6144)` — 1920-dim, 6144 positions at 32 bp.
- Input: **524,288 bp = 524 kb DNA** per forward pass (Extended Data Fig. 1, top label). They keep Borzoi's input window unchanged.
- They add a trainable `(1920 → 4096 → 1920)` position-wise MLP with GELU on top of Borzoi's penultimate embedding (Methods "Model" — *"we introduced a trainable layer with Gaussian error linear unit nonlinearity on top of Borzoi's penultimate layer"*). This is *new* trainable capacity that lives between Borzoi and the cell decoder.

### Per-cell conditioning — the actual borrow target

This is the most architecturally interesting piece. scooby is a **hypernetwork** (Methods "Model" and Extended Data Fig. 1):

- Cell embedding `e_c` ∈ ℝ¹⁴ from Poisson-MultiVI (scvi-tools, latent=14; Methods "Generation of cell embeddings").
- A small MLP `14 → 64 → 128 → 256 → (1920+1) × num_tracks` (Extended Data Fig. 1 right side) produces, for each cell, the **filter weights and bias of a position-wise 1×1 conv** applied to the (1920, 6144) sequence embedding.
- For stranded RNA: `num_tracks = 2`, MLP output is reshaped to `(1921, 2, 1)`. For ATAC: `(1921, 1, 1)`.
- Per-bp prediction = `conv1×1(seq_embedding; weights = MLP(e_c))`.

So cell conditioning is **a hypernetwork generating the final-layer convolution**, not a FiLM, not an additive embedding, not a cross-attention. It's effectively *"the cell tells the model how to read out the sequence embedding."* The MLP is shared; only the cell embedding varies.

### Where does cell conditioning enter

**Output head only.** The Borzoi backbone is cell-agnostic. This is by design (Methods "Model"): the sequence embedding is *cached* per region so that "predicting profiles for multiple cells from the same genomic region" is cheap. They explicitly call this out as one of two optimizations (Methods "Model": *"we introduced a caching mechanism for the sequence embeddings, reducing redundant computations when predicting profiles for 64 randomly sampled cells in a multi-task learning fashion"*).

This is a strong architectural commitment and the obvious lever sc_nona must consider: if you want donor-conditioning that *changes how DNA is read* (e.g., because the donor's genome itself is different), output-head-only conditioning is the wrong place. You'd need conditioning in the backbone — or, more naturally, *give the model the donor's actual sequence* (which sc_nona is already implicitly doing via per-donor genome alignment, though scooby gets reference sequence at inference, not personal sequence).

### LoRA — the other parameter-efficient trick

- **LoRA rank-8** applied to Borzoi's Conv1D layers, separable Conv1D skip connections, and transformer Q/V/MLP projections (Extended Data Fig. 1 left, all four blue boxes have LoRA tags). They use their adapted `peft` fork (`github.com/lauradmartens/peft`) on top of Borzoi.
- LoRA weights merge back into the original weights post-training: **zero inference overhead** (Methods "Model" — *"these matrices can be merged into the existing weights after training, resulting in no overhead during model inference"*).
- Two learning rates: 4×10⁻⁴ for the cell-conditioned conv head, **2×10⁻⁴ for LoRA + the new MLP**, AdamW, warmup 1000 steps, linear decay over 40 epochs (Methods "Training procedure"). LoRA-only ablation (Extended Data Fig. 5) shows LoRA matters — disabling it drops profile-bin Pearson R from ~0.66 to ~0.65 and across-cell-types count R from ~0.55 to ~0.52.

### Combinatorial blowup (cells × sequence)

Two tricks (Methods "Model"):

1. **Cache the sequence embedding** per region, then loop over cells using just the conv head — `O(seq) + O(n_cells × seq × dim)` instead of `O(n_cells × full forward)`.
2. **Decode only at exonic bins** for gene-count evaluation: *"we performed cell-state-specific decoding for expression only on the embedding slices that overlap with exons (or gene body) of interest."* For training they sample 64 cells per region per batch (Methods "Training procedure": *"randomly sampled cells in a multi-task learning fashion"*).

### Loss, training schedule

- **Inherited Borzoi Poisson + multinomial loss** with original weighting (Methods "Training procedure" — *"the same weighting scheme for the Poisson and multinomial loss terms"*). No new loss.
- Gradient clip 0.2, weight decay 10⁻⁶.
- Batch 8 sequences across 8 NVIDIA A40 GPUs, mixed precision, ~2 days to convergence on the NeurIPS multiome dataset (page 2276 col 1: *"63,683 cells … across eight NVIDIA A40 GPUs for 2 days until convergence"*).
- Frozen batchnorm + dropout-disabled in Borzoi at start of training to stabilize the first step, then unfrozen (Methods "Training procedure"). This is a small but lift-able detail.

### Borrowability for MSA-style cross-donor attention

The hypernetwork pattern is **donor-conditionable in exactly the same way it's cell-conditionable** — just swap `e_c` for `e_donor`. That's a free borrow.

But scooby has **no cross-cell or cross-track attention anywhere**. Each cell's prediction is computed independently from a shared sequence embedding. So the MSA idea — attention across rows (donors) at fixed sequence position — has no analog. sc_nona would be inventing this on its own; scooby contributes only the *outer loop* (use a cell/donor embedding to condition a sequence-conditioned model) and the *training-data plumbing* (sparse per-cell coverage matrix).

Concretely: if you stack `n_donor` per-donor coverage tracks at every sequence position and run attention vertically, that's a different architecture than scooby's. The closest scooby gets is the multi-cell sampling per batch — but those cells share the sequence embedding only by caching, not by attending to each other.

## 3. Code, data, resources

### Code (Code availability section, page 2283)

- **Main repo**: `github.com/gagneurlab/scooby` — model, training scripts, data loaders.
- **Reproducibility repo**: `github.com/gagneurlab/scooby_reproducibility` — Jupyter notebooks for the figures.
- **Adapted SnapATAC2 (Rust)**: `github.com/lauradmartens/SnapATAC2` — *this is the single most directly useful piece of code for sc_nona*. It's a Rust crate (rustup v1.28.1, rustc v1.85.0) implementing the sparse per-cell coverage storage with split-read parsing and modified `sp.pp.make_fragment_file(xf_filter=True, is_paired=False, both_shift_left=0, both_shift_right=0)`. The fact that they had to fork SnapATAC2 to make it RNA-aware tells you scooby's data preprocessing is the *non-trivial* engineering of the paper, not the model.
- **Adapted PEFT**: `github.com/lauradmartens/peft` (LoRA on Conv1D).
- **Borzoi-PyTorch**: `github.com/johahi/borzoi-pytorch` (not by scooby authors but they cite it as the base, ref 52: Hingerl, Karollus & Gagneur, *Bioinformatics* 41, btaf467 (2024) — same first author, Borzoi-PyTorch port published as a separate tools paper).

### Data (Data availability section, page 2283)

- NeurIPS hematopoiesis multiome (training): SRP356158, AWS `s3://openproblems-bio/public/post_competition/multiome/`, GEO GSE194122.
- Epicardioids: SRP359250, SRP359249.
- OneK1K (1.2M PBMC scRNA-seq from 982 donors!): SRP359840. **Note**: this is bigger than HipSci on cell count *and* donor count, but it's 10x 3'. Not Smart-seq2.
- Processed pre-computed embeddings + counts on Zenodo (10.5281/zenodo.15517064, 10.5281/zenodo.15517072).
- Model weights: not separately listed; presumably in the main repo or Zenodo.

### What sc_nona can lift directly

1. **The SnapATAC2 RNA fork** — drop in, replace the BAM-loading step with Smart-seq2 BAMs, and you have per-cell sparse storage immediately. The split-read handling (Extended Data Fig. 2) is *more* valuable for Smart-seq2 than for 10x.
2. **The on-the-fly coverage track generator** (Methods "Data preparation for training") — pure function from sparse matrix + cell list + region → (batch, cells, strands, length) tensor. Lift this verbatim.
3. **The Borzoi-PyTorch + LoRA + cell-conditioned conv head architecture** — even if sc_nona ultimately wants a different conditioning strategy, this is the strongest single-cell baseline currently published and should be reproduced as a baseline.
4. **The squashed-scale (clip_soft=5) normalization** — directly transferable. Note: this is *not* CPM or log1p; it's a Borzoi-specific soft-clipping that the user should adopt unless they have a strong reason not to.
5. **The exonic-bin-only decoding trick** for gene-count evaluation — 10× speedup at inference for any expression-aggregation evaluation.

### Notable absence

There is **no "make BigWigs from scRNA reads" standalone CLI**. The pipeline is integrated into the training loop via the SnapATAC fork. For sc_nona's plan (precompute per-donor diploid/hap1/hap2 BigWigs, then feed to model), the user will need to either (a) export per-cell coverage to BigWig via `sp.ex.export_coverage` (scooby uses this only for pseudobulks) or (b) adapt the SnapATAC sparse format to per-donor instead of per-cell.

## 4. Evaluations

### Benchmarks

scooby's evaluations are organized around five claims (Results section headers, pages 2276–2282):

1. **Single-cell profile prediction**: Pearson R 0.15 (single-cell vs scooby) → 0.63 (100-nearest-neighbor avg vs scooby) for RNA; 0.11 → 0.70 for ATAC (Fig. 1d). Always *better than pseudobulk baseline* (0.09 RNA, 0.08 ATAC) but never approaching the noise ceiling (0.7 RNA, 0.7 ATAC).
2. **Cell-type-specific gene expression**: mean Pearson 0.82–0.88 across-gene per cell type (Fig. 2d). 0.86 mean vs. 0.86 for bulk Borzoi (Methods, Extended Data Fig. 4: scooby 0.871 vs seq2cells 0.773 — significant).
3. **Generalization to held-out cell states**: ablate normoblast → predict normoblast from interpolated embeddings → recover 0.79 Pearson R (vs 0.81 with normoblasts in training) (Fig. 2f-g).
4. **TF motif effect scoring**: in-silico mutate PWM-matched binding sites, score effect on predicted expression (Fig. 3a–e). Better correlation with TF expression than chromVAR (P = 5.4×10⁻⁹) and scBasset (P = 0.04) — Extended Data Fig. 6.
5. **Variant effect prediction** (the section sc_nona most needs to engage with) — see below.

### Variant effect prediction (this is the one that matters)

scooby explicitly benchmarks on **eQTLs**:

- **GTEx whole-blood bulk eQTLs**: ±262 kb around TSS, Spearman ρ = 0.449 (scooby) vs ~0.4 (Borzoi track-matched) — Fig. 4a, 4c. Significant improvement (Wilcoxon two-sided P = 0.001).
- **OneK1K cell-type-specific fine-mapped eQTLs** (PIP ≥ 0.9): scooby outperforms Borzoi across all cell types (Fig. 4a) and outperforms seq2cells (Fig. 4b, P = 5×10⁻⁴).
- **Sign concordance**: 78% of fine-mapped eQTL predictions had correct sign when filtered for predicted |fold change| > 3.5% (Fig. 4c).
- **Distance-stratified concordance**: > 90% concordance within ±1 kb of TSS, declining to ~70% beyond 100 kb (Fig. 4d). Same issue as all sequence-based models — distal effects are hard.
- **Cell-type-specific recovery of fine-mapped eQTLs**: scooby beats target-gene-expression and ATAC-peak baselines for top-k cell-type ranking (Fig. 4f).
- **GWAS overlay**: example variant rs143664050 — predicted *TES* expression drop in CD14+ monocytes but not erythroblasts (Fig. 5c), attributed to disruption of an SPI1 motif (Fig. 5d). Shows the model can produce *mechanistic* cell-type-specific hypotheses for GWAS hits.

### Per-cell conditioning vs pseudobulk

Yes, this is explicitly shown. Ablation (Extended Data Fig. 5): "Pseudobulk model" (Borzoi-style, no cell conditioning) achieves count-gene-level Pearson ~0.85, scooby achieves ~0.87. **For across-cell-type count comparison** (the metric that captures cell-state-specific regulation, after subtracting gene mean), scooby ~0.55 vs pseudobulk ~0.48. So the cell-conditioning helps modestly on gene-level expression and meaningfully on cell-state-specific expression patterns.

### What scooby does NOT evaluate

- **No splicing benchmark** (e.g., SpliceAI tasks). Discussion explicitly: *"the 10x scRNA-seq 3′ coverage bias limits the signal for splice sites and TSS choice. Furthermore, we observed little evidence for differential isoform usage in the dataset"* (page 2283). **This is the gap sc_nona's full-length Smart-seq2 substrate is uniquely suited to fill.**
- **No promoter-enhancer pairing benchmark** specifically (CRISPRi-FlowFISH, ENCODE-rE2G, K562 perturbation benchmarks). The TF motif effect score is the closest thing, but it's evaluated by TF-expression correlation, not by held-out enhancer-perturbation data.
- **No long-range distal-regulatory evaluation** beyond the eQTL distance stratification, which itself shows performance degrades > 100 kb from TSS (Fig. 4d, Extended Data Fig. 9c-d).
- **No multi-donor or population-scale variant effect benchmark with per-donor genomes** — eQTLs are inferred from population data but evaluated by in-silico ref-vs-alt substitution at hg38 coordinates, not by aligning to each donor's actual genome. *This is the cleanest niche for sc_nona.*

## 5. Honest comparison

### What scooby does that sc_nona should do too (concrete borrows)

1. **LoRA-finetune a pretrained Borzoi (or NTv3/Evo2)** rather than train from scratch — Extended Data Fig. 5 shows no-LoRA significantly underperforms scooby on both gene-level and across-cell-type metrics; this is a settled finding.
2. **Use a hypernetwork on Borzoi's penultimate embedding for per-{cell, donor} conditioning** — even if sc_nona's final architecture has cross-donor attention, the hypernetwork pattern is the right baseline and the right "fast inference" target.
3. **Adopt Borzoi's squashed-scale (clip_soft=5) normalization, Poisson+multinomial loss, 32-bp bin, 524-kb input**. There's no win to deviating on these unless sc_nona has data the existing Borzoi recipe can't represent.
4. **Sparse-matrix per-cell storage with split-read support** — fork the lauradmartens/SnapATAC2 directly. The matrix abstraction works for Smart-seq2.
5. **Evaluate on cell-type-specific eQTLs** (OneK1K, GTEx) — even though sc_nona is built around HipSci donors, the eQTL benchmarks are the standard the field will demand. Bake this into the eval suite from day one.
6. **Aggregate-over-100-nearest-neighbors as the de-facto ground truth for single-cell evaluations** (Fig. 1c-d) — single-cell signal is too sparse to score directly, and the field has converged on this.

### What sc_nona does that scooby doesn't (your differentiation)

1. **Full-length Smart-seq2 coverage** — scooby explicitly notes 10x's 3' bias as a limitation for splicing/TSS choice (page 2283 Discussion). sc_nona can target splicing, isoform usage, alternative TSS — none of which scooby evaluates.
2. **Per-donor genome alignment with N-masking + WASP + SNPsplit** — scooby aligns everything to plain hg38. Their variant effect prediction is *in-silico ref-vs-alt substitution at hg38*, never read-evidence at per-donor coordinates. sc_nona's haplotype-resolved tracks give direct ASE-style readouts that scooby's framework can't produce.
3. **Variation-across-genotypes as the modeled axis**, not variation-across-cell-states. scooby treats donor as a *batch effect* (Methods "Generation of cell embeddings": *"incorporating sample information as the batch key during data integration"*). sc_nona treats donor as the *signal*.
4. **MSA-style cross-donor attention** — no analog in scooby. This is sc_nona's bet on a genuinely new architecture.
5. **Hap1/hap2/diploid-sum as 3 parallel tracks per cell** — scooby has 1 track (RNA, stranded → 2 values per cell). Tripling the output channel space carries information scooby's tracks fundamentally cannot.

### What's genuinely overlapping / where to be careful

- **Per-cell expression prediction.** scooby owns this metric, with 63,683 cells and 1.2M cells (OneK1K) on tap. sc_nona's HipSci data is ~100–200 donors, tens of cells per donor for Smart-seq2 (Cuomo 2020: ~700 cells across 125 lines; Jerber 2021: ~200k cells but 10x). **The numbers don't favor sc_nona for raw cell-level prediction.** Don't frame sc_nona as "scooby for Smart-seq2"; frame it as "the only model that sees per-donor diploid sequence."
- **eQTL benchmarking.** scooby has done the OneK1K cell-type-specific work thoroughly. sc_nona's eQTL evaluation must add something — most naturally, it should evaluate on HipSci-derived eQTLs (Cuomo / Jerber published eQTLs are the natural target) and *demonstrate the per-donor alignment matters* by ablating to a plain-hg38-aligned variant of the same data.
- **TF motif effect scoring.** scooby has built and validated this (Fig. 3). sc_nona inherits the methodology essentially for free.

### Reframing recommendation

Don't position sc_nona as a single-cell expression model. Position it as: **"a DNA language model that learns RNA-level causal structure (covariation, allele-specific regulation, isoform usage) from full-length single-cell RNA data aligned to personal genomes — a substrate scooby's pipeline cannot represent."** The model is downstream; the data substrate is the differentiator.

## Concrete checklist (do these in sc_nona)

1. **Fork `lauradmartens/SnapATAC2`** and replace the 10x BAM input with a Smart-seq2 BAM loader. Extend the sparse matrix from `(cell × position, value=read_length×strand)` to `(cell × haplotype × position, value=read_length×strand)`. This is the single biggest borrowable engineering artifact in scooby.
2. **Reproduce the scooby baseline on HipSci first**, before building anything new. Train scooby's architecture (Borzoi + LoRA-8 + hypernetwork conv head) on HipSci Smart-seq2 aligned to plain hg38. This (a) validates the data pipeline, (b) gives sc_nona a real baseline number, (c) tells you whether the cell-count gap vs scooby's NeurIPS dataset is actually fatal.
3. **Then ablate to per-donor genomes.** Train the same model on per-donor-aligned tracks (hg38 coords → donor coords via lift-over for evaluation). The headline result needs to be "per-donor alignment improves variant effect prediction by X over plain hg38 on a matched eQTL benchmark." Without this, the personalized-genome work is unjustified.
4. **Add a splicing/isoform evaluation** — pick a benchmark scooby explicitly identified as out of reach (page 2283: splice sites, TSS choice, isoform usage). Smart-seq2 + per-bp coverage is the only thing in the literature that can plausibly do this single-cell. Don't leave this gap on the table.
5. **For MSA-style cross-donor attention**: prototype as a *second-stage* head on top of scooby's frozen output — at each 32-bp bin, you have `(n_donor, 3 tracks)` predicted vs. observed values; run attention vertically across donors, predict the residual. This isolates the contribution of cross-donor attention without rebuilding the backbone.
6. **Use HOCOMOCO v12 PWMs + the TF motif effect score methodology** (Methods "Motif deletion experiments") verbatim for any TF-activity evaluation. No reason to reinvent this.
7. **Pre-commit to the Borzoi-inherited normalization (squashed scale, clip_soft=5), 32-bp bins, 524-kb input, Poisson+multinomial loss, AdamW with 4×10⁻⁴ / 2×10⁻⁴ split learning rates.** Deviating from these without strong reason just adds risk; scooby has shown they work for the single-cell setting.
