### Why the 23 are missing — they are EGA managed-access

Two converging lines of evidence:

1. **Date prefix pattern.** Querying BioSamples for these donors, every one of them has a HipSci line code with an early collection date: `HPSI0513`, `HPSI0613`, `HPSI0713`, `HPSI0514`, `HPSI0614`, `HPSI0714`. The HipSci data-portal paper (Streeter et al. 2017, PMC5210631) states verbatim:

   > "Data from openly consented healthy samples that is uniquely associated with the sample donor, such as genotype or genomic sequence data, can be made freely available to all users through the European Nucleotide Archive (ENA)... For managed-access cell lines, [data] are archived in the EGA."

2. **Cuomo paper's own dual accession.** The Cuomo et al. 2020 (PMC7010688) Data Availability statement reads:
   > "Single cell RNA-seq data are available under the accession numbers **ERP016000 (ENA project)** and **EGAS00001002278, EGAD00001005741 (EGA project)**."

EGAD00001005741 is "Controlled access" with 13,433 CRAM samples under the Wellcome Sanger DAC (verified on `ega-archive.org/datasets/EGAD00001005741`). This dual deposition is the standard HipSci pattern for cohorts that mix consented and managed-access donors.

3. **Negative ENA confirmation.** A free-text ENA search across all of ENA for "analysis" records whose `description` or `sample_alias` mentions e.g. `cicb` returned **zero hits**. The donor's primary fibroblast biosample (`SAMEA2399115` = `HPSI0713pf-cicb`) is PUBLIC in BioSamples (so the donor itself is registered openly), but **no WGS analysis is published in ENA for cicb** — it exists only in EGA.

## HipSci access tiering — how it actually works

| Aspect | Open access tier | Managed access tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Consent class** | "Open consent" — healthy volunteers recruited later in the project under updated consent | "Managed consent" — (a) ALL rare-disease donors (Bardet–Biedl, monogenic diabetes, Alport, Kabuki, retinitis pigmentosa, …) AND (b) all healthy donors collected **early** in the project, i.e. roughly HPSI0513–HPSI0714 lines |
| **Where genome/WGS data live** | ENA `PRJEB15299` (ERP017015), individually downloadable from `ftp.sra.ebi.ac.uk` with no auth | EGA `EGAS00001001465` (umbrella study) + per-cohort datasets `EGAD0000100xxxx` — require HipSci DAC application |
| **Where scRNA / bulk RNA-seq live** | ENA `ERP016000` (Cuomo scRNA), `ERP007111` (HipSci bulk RNA) | EGA `EGAS00001002278` / `EGAD00001005741` (Cuomo scRNA managed half); EGA `EGAS00001000593`, `EGAS00001001137` (bulk RNA managed) |
| **Where chip-imputed genotypes live** | `ftp.hipsci.ebi.ac.uk/hipsci/ftp/data/vep_openaccess_bcf/chr{1..22,X,Y,MT}.bcf` (joint, multi-sample, 328 lines) | Inside the same EGA managed studies |
| **Where proteomics + cellular phenotyping live** | All open, regardless of consent class (these are not uniquely linkable to donor) | n/a |
| **What "PUBLIC" actually means in ENA** | The file is anonymously fetchable over HTTPS, no DAC | A managed-access donor's *cell-line BioSample record* may still be PUBLIC (a stub that exists for cross-reference), but their sequence-level data is only in EGA |

**Why a donor with public BioSample can still have managed-access WGS:** the donor's "fibroblast cell line" biosample is registered openly so that other open data (proteomics, cellular phenotyping, EBiSC catalog) can reference it. But the *sequence assays performed on that biosample* are deposited into EGA, not ENA, if the donor's consent class is managed. This is why my BioSamples query for `SAMEA2399115` (HPSI0713pf-cicb) returned `"status": "PUBLIC"` even though there is no open WGS for cicb.

**Fraction open vs managed in HipSci as a whole:** The data-portal paper (status as of Aug 2016) lists ~477 healthy lines + 86 rare-disease lines. Today's open-access joint BCF lists **328 iPSC lines** (with the 21 Cuomo "missing" donors among the lines that are NOT in it). That is consistent with "~328 of ~563 = ~58% of all HipSci lines are open access" — but only as a rough estimate, since (a) the BCF only includes iPSC, not fibroblasts, and (b) more lines may have been added or removed since 2018.

## Edge cases I checked

- **`iezw` and `uenn`** appear in the open-access imputed BCF (so they are open-consent donors) but have no WGS in PRJEB15299. Plausibly, no WGS was performed for these two donors (the WGS programme covered a subset of the HipSci cohort). For these, the imputed chip BCF is the only open genotype source.
- **`giju`** has only an iPSC-clone WGS gVCF, no parent-fibroblast WGS. Acceptable as a fallback — the iPSC clone is the cell line that was actually used in Cuomo et al. anyway, so the germline vs iPSC distinction matters mainly if you want to avoid reprogramming-induced somatic mutations (~few hundred SNVs per clone, mostly very rare).
- **BioSamples API:** Cuomo's per-cell biosamples (e.g. `SAMEA4029023`) return HTTP 403 "private and not available for browsing" because the run-level metadata for the managed-access half is not exposed. This is consistent with mixed open/managed deposition.
- **The previous agent's claim of "329 iPSC lines" in the open BCF:** I read the BCF header directly via `pysam.VariantFile` → **328 samples**, not 329. Minor count error.

## Practical recommendation: what you can actually get with zero applications

**Realistic plan for the DNA-LM project given the verified access landscape:**

1. **Use the 104 open-access Cuomo donors as your effective cohort.** Cross-checking against the published Cuomo Smart-seq2 FASTQs: the open-access half of the scRNA-seq goes to `ERP016000` (ENA, open), the managed half to EGA. You'd be discarding the EGA half of the scRNA-seq for the 21 managed-access donors anyway, so the 83.2% effective cohort is naturally consistent across all data types.
2. **Genotype source per donor:**
   - 101 donors → use the open `pf` fibroblast WGS gVCF from `PRJEB15299` (preferred — best variant resolution, germline)
   - 1 donor (`giju`) → use its open `i-` iPSC gVCF
   - 2 donors (`iezw`, `uenn`) → fall back to the open-access joint imputed BCF (`vep_openaccess_bcf/`)
3. **Use the open ASE tarball** (`ase_aggregated_by_donor_open_access_lines.tar.gz`, 844 MB, md5 verified) — its 104 Cuomo donors match exactly the 104 covered above.
4. **Do not waste effort on the 21 managed-access donors** unless you specifically want them. To get them you would need to:
   - Apply to the **HipSci Data Access Committee** via EGA (the relevant umbrella study is `EGAS00001001465`; Cuomo's controlled scRNA-seq dataset is `EGAD00001005741`).
   - Sign a Data Access Agreement.
   - Typical turnaround is weeks to months. The DAC may decline applications that don't have a clearly relevant research purpose (a DNA-LM training project should qualify, but it's not automatic).
   - You'd then re-derive the ASE yourself by re-running the cell-genotype-deconvolution pipeline on the controlled scRNA-seq CRAMs against managed-access genotype VCFs.

**Storage budget for the recommended 104-donor open cohort:**

- 104 × ~10 GB per WGS gVCF = ~1 TB raw (most positions are HOM-REF; can be pre-filtered to ~500 MB of actual variant calls per donor → ~52 GB after filter)
- Joint imputed BCF: ~6 GB total (covers the 2 fall-back donors)
- ASE tarball: 844 MB
- scRNA-seq FASTQs from `ERP016000`: dominant disk cost, planned separately
- Personalised diploid STAR indexes (104 × ~6 GB): ~620 GB
