# Variable Cell-Depth Strategies for scRNA-Augmented DNA Language Models

**Setting.** AlphaFold2/3-style stack of `D` aligned smart-seq scRNA coverage tracks over a genomic window `L = 10k–100k+`, with DNA. Need axial / MSA-style attention that is permutation-invariant on the cell axis and can capture cross-cell covariation (= regulatory pair signal). **`D` is upper-bounded at 100** (data constraint).

**Operating constraint (load-bearing).** With `D ≤ 100`, full column attention is `O(D²) ≤ 10,000` ops per position per head — trivial. The cell axis is no longer the compute bottleneck; the genome axis is. This **collapses the design space**: most of the variable-depth bottleneck tricks (ISAB, Perceiver-IO, Linformer-on-set) exist to handle `D` in the thousands, and at `D = 100` they are strictly more expensive than full attention. Pad + mask to `D_max = 100` becomes the simplest workable option.

**Framing caveat.** Protein MSA rows are redundant homologs with phylogenetic correlation — AF2 subsamples partly because rows are redundant. scRNA rows are independent biological replicates carrying fresh statistical signal. Subsampling for protein MSAs is approximately free; for scRNA it throws away the signal you paid for. With `D ≤ 100` you don't have enough cells to throw any away — use all of them.

## TL;DR

1. **Use plain MSA-Transformer-style full column attention.** At `D ≤ 100` the `O(D²)` cost is negligible. No bottleneck (ISAB, Perceiver) needed; it's strictly more expensive than full attention in this regime.
2. **Pad + mask to `D_max = 100`** for variable depth. Simpler than `Uniform[1, n]` subsampling, gives free depth flexibility (a `D=30` sample is 70 masked rows), and you waste at most ~3× compute on the cell axis — itself a rounding error vs. the genome trunk.
3. **No positional encoding on the cell axis** to preserve permutation equivariance.
4. **Compute lives in the genome trunk, not the cell-mixing module.** Adopt AF3's "thin cell-mixing → single track → heavy genomic trunk" template. The trunk is Evo/Borzoi/NTv3-scale; the cell-mixing module is shallow.
5. **Train with random per-step depth `D_actual ∈ [1, 100]`** so the same weights work at `D=10` (rare tissue) and `D=100` (well-sampled). Hold a `D ≤ 20` test set as a calibration guardrail.

## Strategy comparison (in context of `D ≤ 100`)

Notation: `L` genomic length, `D` cells (≤ 100), `d` hidden, `M` bottleneck width.

| # | Strategy | Cell-axis cost | Perm-equivariant? | Cross-cell covariation? | Verdict at D ≤ 100 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pad + mask to D_max=100** | `O(D_max²) = 10k` | yes | yes, full | **RECOMMENDED** — simplest, cheapest |
| 2 | Full column attention with variable `D` (no padding, ragged batch) | `O(D²)` | yes | yes, full | Equivalent to (1) but harder to batch — pick (1) |
| 3 | Subsample to fixed `D_train < 100` | `O(D_train²)` | yes (no row PE) | yes, full | Wastes data you already have; rejected |
| 4 | Perceiver IO cross-attention to `M` latents | `O(D·M)` | yes | partial (one-way) | At `D=100, M=128`: more ops than full attn. Rejected. |
| 5 | Set Transformer ISAB (`M` inducing points) | `O(D·M)` | yes | yes (two-way through bottleneck) | At `D=100, M=128`: 12.8k ops > 10k for full attn, plus extra params. Rejected. |
| 6 | DeepSets pool-then-process | `O(D)` | yes | only first-order unless multi-moment | Cheap baseline / side-channel, not main arch |
| 7 | Recurrent over cells | `O(D)` | not native | weak | Rejected on equivariance |
| 8 | AF3-style OuterProductMean only (no column attn) | `O(D·d²)` mean | yes | weak (single first-order mixing op per block) | Strong **baseline / ablation** — see hybrid 4 below |
| 9 | Linformer on cell axis | `O(D·k)` | **no** | n/a | Breaks equivariance. Rejected. |

### Detailed notes on the still-relevant strategies

**(1) Pad + mask.** This is the right default once `D` is bounded. Fix tensor shape to `D_max = 100`, mask rows where `D_actual < 100`, run full column attention with the mask. Batching is trivial. Wasted compute on padded rows is bounded by a small constant and is dominated by the genome trunk anyway. The original concern with pad+mask ("wasteful if depth distribution is wide") doesn't bite at this scale.

**(6) DeepSets pooling.** Won't capture covariation as a main mechanism — mean-pool throws away the covariance you care about. But it's an excellent ablation baseline ("is the model actually using cross-cell info, or is it just averaging?") and a useful side-channel: concatenate mean + variance + quantiles along `D` to the column-attention output. Bulk-RNA-like summary statistics for free.

**(8) AF3 OuterProductMean baseline.** AF3 replaced column attention with a single `OuterProductMean` op that averages outer products across rows into the pair representation. Drops to `O(D)` on the cell axis, but compresses to first-order cross-cell signal only. AF3 could afford this because (a) protein MSA rows are redundant and (b) the geometric pair representation was doing the heavy lifting downstream. Neither rationale transfers to scRNA. **But** this is the right ablation baseline — if a full column-attention model can't beat an OPM-only model on regulatory tasks, you've learned that cross-cell info beyond the mean isn't helping.

### Why ISAB / Perceiver / Linformer are not appropriate here

ISAB/Perceiver are bottleneck schemes that pay back the bottleneck's parameter overhead via the `O(D·M) ≪ O(D²)` win. The crossover point is `D > M`. At `M = 128` (typical) and `D = 100`, you are below the crossover — the bottleneck is pure overhead with no compute saving and a strict loss in expressivity (rank ≤ M information transfer). Linformer separately requires a canonical row order, which kills permutation equivariance.

Reserve these tools for if you ever scale to `D ≥ 1000` (atlas-scale).

## Recommendation — block-level architecture (annotated)

```
INPUT
─────
  DNA:    (B, L_bp)                          ← B sequences, L_bp base pairs
  scRNA:  (B, D, L_bp)                       ← B sequences × D=100 cells × per-bp coverage
  mask:   (B, D)                             ← which of the 100 cell-slots are real
                                               vs padded


(0) PER-CELL ENCODER
────────────────────
  Goal: turn scalar coverage at each bp into a feature vector at each
        128-bp bin. Same conv weights applied independently to every
        cell (D acts as a batch dim).

  in:   (B, D, L_bp, 1)                      one coverage scalar per bp per cell
  ops:  log1p(mean over 128 bp) + small 1-D conv stack
  out:  (B, D, T, d_cell)                    where T = L_bp/128, d_cell ≈ 256

  Concretely for L_bp=32 kb, D=100, d_cell=256:
        in:  (B, 100, 32768, 1)
        out: (B, 100, 256,   256)


(1) CELL-MIXING BLOCK  × N1 ≈ 4 blocks stacked
──────────────────────
  Goal: let cells share information at the same genomic position,
        and let each cell's information flow along the genome.

  Each block does, in order, on a tensor of shape (B, D, T, d_cell):

    a) ROW attention along T (genome axis)
       — Reshape so D becomes a batch dim: (B*D, T, d_cell)
       — Self-attention along T with RoPE
       — Each cell's coverage track "sees itself" along the genome
       — Reshape back to (B, D, T, d_cell)

    b) COLUMN attention along D (cell axis)
       — Reshape so T becomes a batch dim: (B*T, D, d_cell)
       — Self-attention along D, NO positional encoding (permutation-
         invariant), masked rows ignored
       — At each genomic position, all 100 cells attend to each other
       — Reshape back to (B, D, T, d_cell)

    c) LayerNorm + MLP per (cell, position) vector

  in:  (B, D, T, d_cell)
  out: (B, D, T, d_cell)                     ← same shape, enriched features


(2) CELL-AXIS AGGREGATION
─────────────────────────
  Goal: collapse the D=100 cell axis into a single "consensus" feature
        vector per genomic position. After this step, no more cell
        axis — just one track over the genome.

  How: Set-Transformer-style PMA (Pooling by Multihead Attention).
       Define a small set of learned query vectors (e.g. 1 query):
            q ∈ R^{d_cell}  (or n_queries × d_cell), q is a model PARAMETER
       At every genomic position t independently, cross-attend:
            q  --attends-to-->  the D=100 cell vectors at position t
            weights = softmax(q · v_i / √d) over i = 1..D, masked rows -inf
            output  = sum_i weights_i · v_i
       Output is one (or n_queries averaged) vector per position.

  Mental model: q is a "question" the model has learned to ask about
                cells. Cells whose vectors resemble q get more weight.
                Permutation-invariant on D because softmax-over-keys is.

  Previous art: this is the "PMA" pattern from Set Transformer (Lee et al.
                2019). Equivalent in spirit to BERT [CLS] / ViT class token /
                DETR object queries / Perceiver latent array / Flamingo
                Perceiver Resampler — all "learned-query cross-attention."
                See `planning_mds/7_axial_attention_ntv3_integration.md` §3.4
                for the full explanation + previous-art table + code.

  in:  (B, D, T, d_cell)
  out: (B, T, d_cell)                        ← cell axis is GONE

       Concretely: (B, 100, 256, 256) → (B, 256, 256)


(3) HEAVY GENOMIC TRUNK
───────────────────────
  Goal: do the long-range genomic reasoning (enhancer-promoter etc.)
        on a single track that now contains DNA info AND consensus
        scRNA info.

  Inputs at this point:
        x_dna       (B, T, d_dna)            DNA features at 128 bp resolution
                                             (e.g. NTv3 hidden state post-conv-tower)
        x_consensus (B, T, d_cell)           consensus scRNA track from step (2)
                                             — SAME T, same position index

  Three concrete ways to combine them — pick one based on whether the
  trunk is trained from scratch or fine-tuned from a pretrained checkpoint:

    A) FEATURE-DIM CONCAT
       Stack along the channel axis, then project:
           x = Linear(concat([x_dna, x_consensus], dim=-1))
       in:  two tensors → out: (B, T, d_trunk)
       Trade-off: gives the trunk free access to both signals separately.
       Best when training the trunk from scratch.

    B) ADDITIVE BIAS (recommended when adapting a pretrained trunk like NTv3)
       Project consensus to d_dna, add (zero-init the projection):
           x = x_dna + W·x_consensus                 → (B, T, d_dna)
       Day-0 model = vanilla DNA-only model. Preserves the input
       distribution the pretrained trunk was trained on.

    C) FiLM (multiplicative scale + additive shift, per token)
       Project consensus to (gamma, beta), modulate every trunk
       block's LayerNorm output:
           x = LN(x_dna) * (1 + gamma(x_consensus)) + beta(x_consensus)
       Stronger conditioning than (B). See `planning_mds/7_axial_attention_ntv3_integration.md`
       for the NTv3-specific implementation using `adaptive_layers.py`.

  Then run N2 ≈ 20–40 transformer blocks (or hand off to NTv3's
  6/12-layer transformer stack for that backbone).

  in:  combined (B, T, d_trunk)
  out: (B, T, d_trunk)


(4) HEADS
─────────
  Several outputs from the trunk hidden state, e.g.:
    — DNA MLM logits          (B, L_bp, vocab)        after deconv/upsample
    — Per-cell coverage pred  (B, D, T, 1)            held-out-cell objective
    — Promoter–enhancer pair  (B, T, T)               optional
```

### Quick mental model

- **Step 0** turns each cell's bp coverage into a per-cell feature track at 128 bp resolution.
- **Step 1** lets cells talk to each other (column attention) and lets each cell's track talk to itself along the genome (row attention).
- **Step 2** collapses the cell axis: at each genomic position, attention-pool over the 100 cells to get one consensus vector. The cell axis disappears.
- **Step 3** takes that consensus track + the DNA track (both at the same 128 bp resolution, same position index) and runs the expensive genome-axis transformer on the combined representation.

### Hyperparameters to start with

| Param | Start | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| `D_max` | 100 | Data ceiling |
| `D_actual` distribution at train | `Uniform[1, 100]` per step | Depth-robust weights, AF3-style |
| Cell-mixing blocks `N1` | 4 | Two rounds of cross-cell info flow |
| Genomic trunk blocks `N2` | match Evo/Borzoi | Compute belongs here |
| Row PE on cell axis | none | Permutation invariance |
| Cell metadata (type, donor) | additive conditioning vectors | Conditions on biology without breaking axis invariance |
| Cell mask | yes | Pad to 100, ignore in attention softmax |
| Side channel | mean + variance pool over `D`, concat to trunk input | Free DeepSets baseline / regularizer |

### Why these choices

- **Pad+mask over subsample:** at `D ≤ 100`, full attention on the cell axis costs nothing. Subsampling discards independent biological samples — which is exactly the signal you collected the data for.
- **No cell-axis bottleneck:** ISAB/Perceiver only win when `D > M`. With `D ≤ 100 < 128`, full attention is cheaper.
- **Aggregate to single track before the heavy trunk** (AF3 template): the genome trunk is the expensive module. Running it on `L × d` rather than `D × L × d` is a `~100×` saving with no loss of signal as long as the cell-mixing module did its job.
- **Random `D_actual` at train:** decouples model performance from data availability at inference. Same weights serve `D=10` rare tissue and `D=100` well-sampled tissue. Cheap.
- **Per-cell metadata as additive conditioning:** preserves *axis* permutation invariance (the axis is still a set) while letting individual rows carry cell-type/donor identity. Standard biology-conditioning trick from scooby/Decima.

## Hybrid designs worth considering

1. **Variance side-channel.** Concat `mean(D), var(D), quantiles(D)` pooled along the cell axis to the genomic trunk input. Captures bulk-RNA-like statistics for free; acts as a regularizer if the column attention overfits.
2. **AF3 OuterProductMean ablation.** Replace full column attention with one OuterProductMean per cell-mixing block. If the full-column-attention model can't beat this, cross-cell info beyond first moments isn't buying you anything — important null result.
3. **MSA-Transformer-faithful baseline.** Identical to recommended arch but compare against (i) DNA-only Evo/NTv3 baseline (no scRNA at all) and (ii) scooby-style cell-conditioned decoder (cell axis as condition, not set). These three ablations cleanly attribute any gain to the axial attention.
4. **Pseudobulk-pre-clustered variant.** Cluster cells into `K ≤ 100` pseudobulks (e.g. Leiden on PCA), feed pseudobulks as the rows. Sacrifices intra-cluster heterogeneity for noise reduction. Good ablation: does single-cell resolution beat pseudobulk for promoter–enhancer recovery, or is cell-type granularity enough?
5. **Perceiver-IO output decoder.** If you ever want *per-cell* output predictions, add a Perceiver decoder cross-attention from per-cell query embeddings to the genome trunk output. This is just for output, doesn't change the input-side architecture. Defer until needed.
6. **Future scaling to `D > 100`:** if you later get atlas-scale data, switch the column attention to ISAB (`M = 128`) at that point. The rest of the architecture stays.

## Open empirical questions

1. **Does cross-cell covariation actually improve promoter–enhancer recovery** over a strong DNA-only Borzoi/NTv3 baseline? Core hypothesis of the project. Run the OPM-only and DNA-only ablations.
2. **What's the right pretraining objective?** Masked scRNA-coverage prediction on held-out cells is the natural choice and forces the column-attention module to do real work. Other options (contrastive, autoregressive over cells, denoising) worth piloting.
3. **Does `D_actual` matter beyond training stability?** Specifically: at fixed inference `D=20`, does a model trained with `Uniform[1, 100]` outperform one trained with `Uniform[1, 20]`? Tests whether high-`D` training transfers downward.
4. **Cell-type conditioning vs pure set.** I lean: condition on cell type / donor as additive vectors, keep the axis itself permutation-invariant. Worth ablating against (a) no conditioning and (b) cell-type-as-row-PE (breaks invariance but maybe better fit).
5. **Splicing signal from bp coverage.** Junction-spanning reads create coverage discontinuities — does the model pick up splice signal from raw bp coverage alone, or do you need to add explicit junction tracks (as Borzoi does)?
6. **Behavior at `D = 1`** (no scRNA available, just DNA). `Uniform[1, 100]` training covers this. Test that the model degrades gracefully to a DNA-only model rather than catastrophically.
7. **Cell-axis context length** at the genome end. At `L = 100 kb` × `D = 100`, the cube is 10M positions. Memory budget pushes you to chunk the genome (already standard) and/or reduce per-cell hidden dim in stages (0)-(1) before scaling up to the trunk in (3).
