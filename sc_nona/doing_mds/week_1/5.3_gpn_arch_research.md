## 1. Bottom line on the two key questions

1. **Does either model use axial attention (separate attention along positions vs across MSA rows)?**
   - **GPN-MSA: NO.** The MSA is not a 2D matrix attended to. The 99 (or 89, see below) non-focal species at each position are collapsed into a *single per-position auxiliary vector* (concatenated one-hots), which is concatenated to the focal-nucleotide embedding. The model is then a plain 1D transformer (RoFormer) over `L` positions with standard full self-attention. This is *explicitly different* from MSA Transformer, which the paper notes (see "Differences between GPN-MSA and MSA Transformer" section). [GPN-MSA paper, Methods ŌåÆ "Model Architecture"; HF config; ablation "MSA frequency"]
   - **GPN-Star: PARTIAL / SOMETHING-LIKE-AXIAL, but not standard axial.** It does keep a 2D `(N_T, L)` tensor of target-species embeddings and runs two distinct attention modules per encoder block:
     - **(a) sequence-wise self-attention**, along `L`, applied to each target species, but with queries/keys shared from the primary target (this is borrowed from MSA Transformer's tied row attention)
     - **(b) phylogeny-informed cross-attention**, where each target token at column `j` attends to clade-pooled source embeddings at column `j`, biased by phylogenetic distance
     There is *no column-wise attention across species at the same position* in the MSA Transformer sense. Instead, "across species" mixing happens in step (a) via the attention-pool that compresses source species into clade embeddings, and in step (b) via the distance-biased cross-attention. So calling it "axial attention" is approximately right at a 30,000-ft view but mechanically inaccurate. [GPN-Star paper, Methods ŌåÆ "The GPN-Star encoder", "Sequence-wise self-attention", "Phylogeny-informed cross-attention"]

2. **Is the number of MSA rows fixed or variable?**
   - **GPN-MSA: effectively fixed by architecture.** The auxiliary-feature input has fixed length `n_aux_features = 445 = 89 ├Ś 5` (89 non-human vertebrate species ├Ś 5-state one-hot). The 10 species closest to human were filtered out from the original 100-vertebrate `multiz100way` alignment, leaving 89 aux + 1 focal = 90. Missing species at a position are encoded as a special "gap"/missing one-hot state in the 5-vocab, so the architecture accepts missing alignments by setting those one-hots, not by varying the number of rows. [HF `config.json`: `n_aux_features: 445`, `aux_features_vocab_size: 5`, `vocab_size: 6`; GPN-MSA paper, MSA Processing]
   - **GPN-Star: variable / flexible.** A central design goal of GPN-Star is to "flexibly accommodate alignments of arbitrary composition and size, eliminating the need for manual curation." Species are grouped into clades by single-linkage clustering on phylogenetic distance with a threshold `Žå_clade` (0.2 for distant alignments, 0.05 for close ones). The number of clades `N_C` and number of target species `N_T` are not architecturally fixed. In training, 19 source clades are sampled and one species is drawn per clade ŌåÆ 20 target sequences per training step; at inference only 1 target is used. The total number of source species `N_S` is whatever the WGA provides (e.g. 100 vertebrates, 447 mammals, 243 primates, 124 insects, etc.; see Supp Table 1/8). [GPN-Star paper, Methods ŌåÆ "Training Data", "Target and source sequences", "Grouping species into clades", "Training Setup", "Inference"]

## 2. GPN-MSA architecture

### Inputs and tokenization

- Input: a 128-bp aligned window from the `multiz100way` whole-genome MSA, after stitching MAF blocks, dropping columns with gaps in human, and removing the 10 primates closest to human. So the operative MSA has **90 species: human (focal) + 89 aux**.
- At each of the `L = 128` positions:
  - The **focal (human) token** is one integer in a vocab of size 6 (`vocab_size: 6`), encoding {A, C, G, T, gap/unknown, mask}.
  - The **auxiliary features** at that position are a fixed-length vector of `n_aux_features = 445 = 89 species ├Ś 5 states`. Each of the 89 non-human rows is one-hot-encoded into 5 dimensions (A/C/G/T/gap) and these are concatenated. This is *not* a learned per-species embedding; it is a fixed-format concatenated one-hot.
- These are combined inside the embedding layer. From the repo `gpn/model.py` (legacy GPNRoFormer registration; partial inspection):
  - The focal token is embedded to `embedding_size = 768`.
  - The aux features (length 445) are placed alongside the focal embedding inside the per-position representation, so each position carries both the focal nucleotide signal and the column-wise multi-species evidence as a single vector.
- Net effect: at each of 128 positions the model sees one combined `(focal-embed ŌŖĢ 89-species-onehot)` vector. The MSA is **never attended to as a 2D matrix**.
- The paper makes this explicit: "The 1-hot encodings of nucleotides from different species at each position are *first concatenated*. Then, the sequence of MSA columns is processed through a Transformer neural network (RoFormer) resulting in a high-dimensional contextual embedding of each position." (Methods ŌåÆ Model Architecture)

[CONFIRMED from paper Methods section + HF config.json]

### Backbone

- **RoFormer** (Su et al. 2021, rotary positional embeddings). Standard HuggingFace `RoFormerEncoder`.
- Hyperparameters (from HF `config.json` for `songlab/gpn-msa-sapiens`):
  - `num_hidden_layers: 12`
  - `num_attention_heads: 12`
  - `hidden_size: 768`
  - `embedding_size: 768`
  - `intermediate_size: 3072`
  - `max_position_embeddings: 1536` (note: window is 128bp, so this is generous)
  - `vocab_size: 6` (focal token: A/C/G/T/gap/mask)
  - `n_aux_features: 445`, `aux_features_vocab_size: 5`
  - `hidden_dropout_prob: 0.1`, `attention_probs_dropout_prob: 0.1`
  - `hidden_act: "gelu"`
  - Architecture string: `"GPNRoFormerForMaskedLM"`
- Total params: ~86 M (paper Methods, "approximately 86 million").

[CONFIRMED]

### Attention pattern

- **Standard full self-attention along the `L = 128` sequence axis.** No row/axial/sparse/local masking. There is no second attention dimension. [CONFIRMED ŌĆö RoFormer backbone is vanilla self-attention.]

### Training objective and loss

- **Masked language modeling (BERT-style)**, but with several wrinkles:
  - **Only the focal (human) token is masked**, never the aux species. (This is opposite to MSA Transformer, which masks across the alignment.) [CONFIRMED, paper "Differences between GPN-MSA and MSA Transformer".]
  - 15% of positions are masked during training.
  - Loss is weighted cross-entropy with weights `w ŌłØ (0.1┬Ę1{repeat} + 1{┬¼repeat}) ┬Ę max(phyloP,1) ┬Ę (phastCons_M + 0.1)`, downweighting repeats and upweighting conserved regions. `phastCons_M` is the smoothed max of phastCons in a 7-bp window.
  - Data augmentation: in non-conserved positions (phastCons_M < 0.1), the reference is replaced by a random nucleotide with probability `q = 0.5` *before computing loss*, to push the model toward uniform/neutral predictions in non-functional regions.
- Output: per-masked-position softmax over {A,C,G,T} (4-way), trained on reference. Variant score = `log[P(ALT)/P(REF)]`, averaged over forward/reverse strand.
- Training data: top 5% most conserved (phastCons 75th percentile in 128bp windows) plus 0.1% random. chr21 held out for early stopping, chr22 held out for testing.
- Training compute: 4├ŚA100, ~4.75 h. Scoring throughput: ~5 M variants/h.

[CONFIRMED]

### Tensor shapes (for the viz)

- Input per example:
  - `input_ids`: `(B, 128)` ŌĆö focal-species token IDs, vocab 6
  - `aux_features`: `(B, 128, 445)` ŌĆö concatenated one-hots over 89 species ├Ś 5 states
- Embedding output: `(B, 128, 768)`
- After 12 RoFormer layers: `(B, 128, 768)`
- MLM head output: `(B, 128, 4)` softmax over {A,C,G,T} at each position (loss only on masked positions, which during inference is the variant site).

### What the architecture does NOT do (important for the viz)

- It does **not** run any attention across MSA rows.
- It does **not** treat each species as a separate token sequence.
- It does **not** have a separate "row embedding" for each species; species identity is implicit in the fixed position of that species inside the 445-dim aux vector.
- It does **not** explicitly use the phylogenetic tree.
- It does **not** use any RNA or functional genomics data.

### Caveats / uncertainties

- I confirmed the model class is `GPNRoFormerForMaskedLM` via HF config. I could not directly fetch the legacy `model.py` source for `GPNRoFormerModel` (the repo restructured and the canonical file path returned 404), but the partial code I did see in `gpn/model.py` is consistent with the architecture I describe (aux_features concatenated into the per-position representation). [INFERRED-but-strong: matches paper Methods text verbatim.]
- The HF config says `multiz100way/89/...` and `n_aux_features = 89├Ś5 = 445`, which matches the paper's "remove 10 closest primates" ŌåÆ 90 species ŌåÆ 89 aux. So the 100-vertebrate alignment is **subset to 90 species** at training time, but this is a data-processing choice, not architectural ŌĆö you could swap in any fixed set as long as you set `n_aux_features` accordingly.

## 3. GPN-Star architecture

GPN-Star (Genomic Pretrained Network with Species Tree and Alignment Representations) was posted to bioRxiv on 2025-09-21 by Ye, Benegas, Albors, Canal Li, Prillo, Fields, Clarke, Song. It is the same Song-lab line of work as GPN-MSA and explicitly designed to fix its limitations.

### Why a new architecture

The GPN-Star paper diagnoses three problems with GPN-MSA:
1. Uses a generic 1D transformer architecture "suitable for unaligned sequences," failing to exploit the structure of alignment data.
2. Cannot natively handle the evolutionary relationship between species (the phylogeny is invisible to it).
3. Closely related species (primates) deteriorate performance, so they had to be hand-excluded; the resulting model is "highly tuned for a particular alignment."

### High-level structure

GPN-Star is an **encoder-only transformer with a custom 2-tower input and two specialized attention modules per block**:

- **Inputs:**
  - A WGA window. The alignment is `X Ōłł R^{N_S ├Ś L}` where `N_S` = number of species and `L` = window length.
  - From this, two views are constructed:
    - **Target sequences** `{x^(t)}, t Ōłł T`, with `|T| = N_T` species (in training, 20 sampled species; in inference, just 1: the primary target, e.g. human). These are the sequences whose masked nucleotides the model must predict.
    - **Source sequences** `{x^(s)}, s Ōłł S`, with `|S| = N_S` (all species in the alignment). These provide the evolutionary context.
- **Clade grouping:** Source species are partitioned into clades by single-linkage clustering of pairwise phylogenetic distance with threshold `Žå_clade` (0.2 for distant alignments, 0.05 for closely related alignments). Number of clades = `N_C`.

### Embedding

- **Target embedding**: each token `x_j^(t)` is mapped through a learned embedding `f_e: R^V ŌåÆ R^H`, producing `E^(t) Ōłł R^{L ├Ś H}`. Standard. Shapes: one such matrix per target species, stack is `(N_T, L, H)`.
- **Source embedding via attention-pool over species in a clade**: this is the key trick.
  - For each clade `c` and each column `j`, all source species in that clade are pooled into a single embedding using an attention-pool that takes a learned bias `b(Žå_{c,s})` based on the phylogenetic distance between species `s` and the MRCA of clade `c`.
  - Formula: `Attn_pool(x_j^(s), sŌłłc) = ╬Ż_s softmax(x_j^(s) W_╬▒ + b(Žå_{c,s})) ┬Ę x_j^(s) W_v`
  - Outputs across `A` heads are concatenated and projected to `H`-dim.
  - Result: source embeddings `E^(c) Ōłł R^{L ├Ś H}`, stacked across `c` Ōłł `C` ŌåÆ `(N_C, L, H)`.
- This is where the model first reduces dimensionality across species. The "fan-in" of species ŌåÆ clades happens once, before any cross-attention. So variable `N_S` is handled here (any species can be added to its clade).

### Encoder block (├ŚK)

Each block has three components, in order:

1. **Sequence-wise self-attention** (along `L`, applied per target species)
   - Each target species `t` has its own `E^(t) Ōłł R^{L ├Ś H}`.
   - Trick borrowed from **MSA Transformer**: queries and keys are computed from the *primary target species* `t_0` (e.g. human) only, then the attention map is *shared* across all `N_T` target species when computing values.
   - This reduces memory from `O(N_T ┬Ę L┬▓)` to `O(L┬▓)`.
   - Uses **RoPE** (rotary positional encoding) on positions, anchored to the primary target's genomic coordinates.
   - Formula: `Attn_seq(E^(t)) = softmax((E^(t_0) W_Q^seq)(E^(t_0) W_K^seq)^T / ŌłÜD) ┬Ę E^(t) W_V^seq`

2. **Phylogeny-informed cross-attention** (along species, target ŌåÉ clade-source)
   - Queries from target embedding at column `j`, `E_j^(T) Ōłł R^{N_T ├Ś H}`.
   - Keys/values from clade-pooled source embeddings at column `j`, `E_j^(C) Ōłł R^{N_C ├Ś H}`.
   - Adds an evolutionary-distance bias `b(╬”)` where `╬” Ōłł R^{N_T ├Ś N_C}` is the average phylogenetic distance from each target species to source species in each clade.
   - The bias function uses Functional Interpolation for Relative Positional Encoding (FIRE) adapted for evolutionary distances: `b(Žå) = f_╬Ė(g(Žå)/g(Žå_max))` with `g(x) = log(cx+1)` and `f_╬Ė` a two-layer MLP with SiLU.
   - In-clade attention masking: a target species in clade `c` is *not allowed* to attend to its own clade's source embedding (prevents the model from learning a trivial copy from same-clade source).
   - Formula: `Attn_phy(E_j^(T), E_j^(C)) = softmax((E_j^(T) W_Q^phy)(E_j^(C) W_K^phy)^T / ŌłÜD + b(╬”)) ┬Ę E_j^(C) W_V^phy`

3. **Feed-forward** (standard, 2-layer MLP, intermediate size `F`, GELU activation)

Standard residual + LayerNorm + dropout (0.1) throughout.

### Output

- MLM head: `p╠é(x_j^(t) | E_j^(t)) = softmax(W_o ┬Ę E_j^(t) + b_o)`
- Predicts 4-way nucleotide probabilities at each masked target position.

### Sizes (Supplementary Table 9)

| Model size | K (blocks) | H (hidden) | F (FFN) | A_self | A_cross | head dim |
|------------|-----------:|----------:|-------:|-------:|--------:|--------:|
| 25 M       | 8          | 512        | 2048   | 4      | 4       | 64      |
| 85 M       | 12         | 768        | 3072   | 6      | 6       | 64      |
| 200 M      | 16         | 1024       | 4096   | 8      | 8       | 64      |

Dropout 0.1 across all sizes. (Supp Table 9.)

### Training setup (Supplementary Table 1)

Three human-genome models, named by alignment depth:
- **GPN-Star (V)**: `multiz100way` vertebrates, 100 species, `Žå_clade=0.2`, 45 clades, context size **128 bp**, sizes {25M, 85M, 200M}. Largest model trained 4.5 days on 8├ŚA100.
- **GPN-Star (M)**: `cactus447way` mammals, 447 species, `Žå_clade=0.05`, 145 clades, context size **256 bp**.
- **GPN-Star (P)**: same Cactus alignment subset to 243 primates, `Žå_clade=0.05`, 21 clades, context size **256 bp**.

Plus five non-human models: mouse, chicken, fly, *C. elegans*, *A. thaliana*, each with their own alignment.

Training:
- Sample 19 target clades (with/without replacement) per training step, then one species per clade ŌåÆ 20 target species per batch, plus the primary target.
- Mask 15% of non-gap positions in each target sequence. Same positions are masked in same-clade species (to prevent leakage).
- 90% [MASK] / 10% leave-as-is (no random replacement, unlike standard BERT). Loss is computed only on masked tokens.
- Same loss weighting as GPN-MSA: `w ŌłØ (0.1┬Ę1{repeat} + 1{┬¼repeat}) ┬Ę max(phyloP,1) ┬Ę (phastCons_M + 0.1)`.
- Data augmentation: replace reference with random nucleotide with probability 0.5 in high-confidence neutral sites (phastCons_M < `p_neutral`).
- Optimizer: not stated explicitly here. Learning rate 5e-5 for 200M, 1e-4 for 85M/25M. 200K steps for V; 150K for M/P.

### Tensor shapes (for the viz)

- Source side: `(B, N_S, L)` ŌåÆ embedded ŌåÆ pooled-by-clade ŌåÆ `(B, N_C, L, H)` per layer
- Target side: `(B, N_T, L)` ŌåÆ embedded ŌåÆ `(B, N_T, L, H)` per layer
- Throughout encoder blocks, both `(N_C, L, H)` and `(N_T, L, H)` shapes are maintained (the source side is pooled once before block 1, and I believe stays fixed across blocks ŌĆö that's what the Methods description implies, but the paper does not explicitly say whether the clade-pooled `E^(C)` is recomputed every layer or computed once).
- Output head: target-only, `(B, N_T, L, 4)` softmax probabilities. At inference `N_T = 1`.

[INFERRED uncertainty: I'm 90% sure source embeddings are computed once (before block 1) and then re-used as cross-attention K/V in every block. The Methods text describes the attention pool as a module that "compresses N_S ŌåÆ N_C," suggesting it's once. But I cannot 100% confirm without reading `gpn/star/`.]

### Inference and scoring

- At inference, only **one** target sequence is used (the primary species ŌĆö e.g. human). All source species are used.
- Score the variant site by masking the center of the context window:
  - LLR = `log[p(alt) / p(ref)]`
  - Entropy = `ŌłÆ╬Ż p(x) log p(x)`
- **Mutation-rate calibration** (new in GPN-Star): subtract `LLR_neutral(x, alt)` computed from neutral sites binned by pentanucleotide context. This is required because models trained on observed sequences inherently capture both selection and mutation rate; calibration isolates selection.

### What's NEW vs GPN-MSA (for the viz)

1. **All target species are masked & predicted** during training, not just human. (Ōē½ more training signal.)
2. **Phylogeny is explicit**, encoded via the clade structure + the distance-biased attention bias.
3. **Variable, modular alignment input**: arbitrary `N_S` and arbitrary species composition.
4. **Tied row attention** (├Ā la MSA Transformer) for sequence-wise self-attention across the N_T target species.
5. **Two separate attention modules per block**: sequence-axis + phylogeny-cross.
6. **Mutation-rate calibration** as a post-hoc step on scores.
7. **Three trained timescales** (V/M/P) corresponding to vertebrate/mammal/primate alignments, with different optimal use cases (coding variants ŌåÆ V; non-coding/regulatory ŌåÆ M; primate-specific constraint ŌåÆ P).

## 5. Pointers for the visualization

If the visualization wants to show how the alignment is consumed:

- **For GPN-MSA**, draw the alignment as a `(90, 128)` matrix on the left, then collapse vertically into a single `(128, 445)` aux feature track + a `(128,)` focal sequence track. These two tracks merge into one `(128, 768)` embedding stream. Then 12 RoFormer self-attention blocks operate **along the horizontal axis only**. The vertical (species) dimension is gone after the input embedding.
- **For GPN-Star**, draw the alignment as a `(N_S, L)` matrix on the left, branching into two pipelines:
  - upper pipeline: subset to `N_T` *target* species ŌåÆ `(N_T, L, H)`, kept throughout (vertical dimension persists).
  - lower pipeline: all `N_S` *source* species ŌåÆ attention-pool by clade ŌåÆ `(N_C, L, H)`, kept throughout.
  - In each encoder block: (i) horizontal self-attn on the target tensor (one attention map shared across all `N_T` rows, computed from primary target only); (ii) vertical-ish cross-attn that mixes the target rows with the clade-source rows at each column `j`, biased by phylogenetic distance; (iii) FFN.

Two visual gotchas:

- In GPN-Star, the "vertical axis" of the source tensor is *clades*, not species. The species ŌåÆ clade reduction happens once before block 1.
- In GPN-Star, the "horizontal self-attention" is unusual ŌĆö Q and K come from the primary target only and the attention map is broadcast to all target rows. That's a tied-row-attention trick from MSA Transformer.
