## Summary Verdict

**The biggest publicly available *full-coverage single-cell* dataset with genotype variation is Cuomo et al. 2020** (ENA: PRJEB14362, **125 donors, ~36K Smart-seq2 cells**, iPSC → definitive endoderm, paired WGS). Jerber et al. 2021 is larger by donor count (215) and cell count (>1M) but uses **10x Chromium 3' v2**, which is *not* full coverage. None of the non-Smart-seq full-length single-cell protocols (Smart-seq3, FLASH-seq, VASA-seq, MAS-seq, FLAMES, SnISOr-Seq) yet have published cohorts that exceed Cuomo on the combined axis of (donors × cells × paired WGS × full coverage).

**What is *new and worth adding* to the inventory:**

1. **A genuinely large long-read single-cell sc-eQTL cohort exists now**: Li et al. 2026 (bioRxiv, NCI/Korea lung) — **129 never-smoker Korean women, isoform-resolved scRNA-seq, paired genotype, sc-isoQTL mapping in 37 cell types**. This is the first long-read single-cell dataset with sample size approaching Jerber and the only one with formal isoQTL design. ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.03.27.714873))

2. **TenK10K Phase 1 already has data on disk**: ~5M cells × 1,925 donors with matched WGS — but it is 10x Chromium 5'/3' chemistry, *not* full coverage. Listed for scale-comparison, not as a Smart-seq alternative.

3. **The big "full coverage × genotype" wins are in bulk RNA-seq**: GTEx (~948 donors × ~54 tissues, ~17K samples), Geuvadis (462 donors), MAGE (731 1000G donors across 26 populations), eQTLGen (31,684 individuals meta-analysis), and the GTEx long-read complement (Glinos et al. 2022, Oxford Nanopore on 88 GTEx samples spanning 15 tissues). For a DNA LM whose hard constraint is "what does sequence variation do to expression / splicing," these bulk cohorts dominate single-cell on donor count by 1–2 orders of magnitude.

4. **VASA-seq, FLASH-seq, Smart-seq3, Smart-seq-total, MAS-seq, FLAMES, scISOr-Seq, SnISOr-Seq2 all currently lack any large multi-donor public cohort.** They exist almost exclusively as method-paper datasets (small panels, 1–10 donors) or as small disease-focused studies. The Mereu et al. pancreas atlas (Oct 2025 preprint, 57 donors, multi-platform including VASA-seq) is the closest to a real multi-donor full-length single-cell collection outside Smart-seq2.

## 1. Full-Length Single-Cell Protocols: Best Genotype-Varying Datasets per Protocol

### 1a. Smart-seq3 / Smart-seq3xpress

Smart-seq3 ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-020-0497-0)) and Smart-seq3xpress ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-022-01311-4)) have not been adopted by any large population-scale eQTL cohort as of 2025. Published Smart-seq3 datasets are method-development atlases or single-tissue mouse studies.

| Dataset | Accession | Donors | Cells | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hagemann-Jensen Smart-seq3 method | **E-MTAB-8735** (NOT GSE149224 — wrong) | Multiple cell lines + CAST × B6 F1 mouse fibroblasts | **5,376-cell HCA benchmark + 369 fibroblasts + HEK293FT** | *Nat Biotechnol* 38:708 (2020), PMID 32518404 |
| Hagemann-Jensen Smart-seq3xpress PBMC atlas | **E-MTAB-11488 + E-MTAB-11452 + E-MTAB-11467** (NOT GSE176122 — wrong) | **7 donors** | **26,260** | ***Nat Biotechnol* 40:1452 (2022)** (NOT *Genome Biol* 23:67); PMID 35637418; PBMCs only |
| Grommisch et al. mouse esophagus regional atlas | **GSE254855** | C57BL/6 mice | thousands | *Dev Cell* 60(2):320 (2024), PMID 39426382; Smart-seq3 confirmed |

**Bottom line on Smart-seq3**: no large genotype-varying public cohort exists. The protocol is technically superior to Smart-seq2 (adds 5' UMI) but has not been deployed at iPSC-population scale.

### 1b. FLASH-seq (Hahaut et al. 2022)

FLASH-seq ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-022-01312-3)) is an accelerated Smart-seq3 variant developed at the Institute of Molecular and Clinical Ophthalmology Basel.

VASA-seq ([DOI](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41587-022-01361-8)) is the only commonly cited *total RNA* (non-polyA-restricted) full-length scRNA-seq method, commercially available exclusively through Single Cell Discoveries (Utrecht).

## 2. Bulk RNA-seq with Full Coverage and Massive Genotype Variation

The researcher's stated constraint is "genotype variation × full coverage" — *not* strictly single-cell. If single-cell resolution is relaxed, the donor-count gap closes by 1–3 orders of magnitude. These are the resources that dominate any sequence → expression learning problem at scale.

**Why this matters for a DNA LM**: GTEx (~948 donors × 54 tissues) and MAGE (731 globally diverse donors) provide *exactly* the genotype × expression supervision signal at sample sizes where statistical learning of variant effects becomes tractable. The trade-off is loss of single-cell resolution. For NTv3/Evo2-style training on sequence → expression, bulk likely dominates single-cell on the relevant metric until TenK10K-scale data come online — and even then, bulk full-coverage cohorts will remain the only source of *full-coverage* genotype × expression supervision.

## 5. What is *Missing* from File 4 That This File Adds

In one sentence each:

- **Long-read single-cell with real donor scale now exists**: Li et al. 2026 Korean lung (129 donors) is the new high-water mark for sc-isoQTL with full isoform coverage. Add to top-tier priority.
- **TenK10K Phase 1 has data on disk**: 1,925 donors × ~5M cells already, with matched WGS. Phase 2 → 10,000 donors. But 10x chemistry only.
- **Bulk full-coverage cohorts are the real volume play**: GTEx + Glinos long-read + MAGE + Geuvadis + eQTLGen + PsychENCODE + TOPMed collectively offer 10,000+ donors of full-coverage transcript data with paired genotype. For a DNA LM, this is where the supervision signal scales.
- **VASA-seq has its first multi-donor human deployment** (Mereu et al. 2025 pancreas, 57 donors) — track as it publishes.
- **Smart-seq3, FLASH-seq, Smart-seq-total have not scaled** — no population-scale cohorts to add.
- **MAS-seq has not been deployed for sc-eQTL** as of August 2025. Korean lung uses Nanopore, not PacBio.
- **Mouse strain long-read single-cell does not yet exist at scale**. Collaborative Cross / DO have only bulk RNA-seq.

## 6. Caveats and What to Verify

### Accession verification
All accession numbers retrieved via web search and PubMed citation. Verify before download:
- GEO: ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/
- ArrayExpress / BioStudies: ebi.ac.uk/biostudies/
- ENA: ebi.ac.uk/ena/
- EGA (controlled access, application required): ega-archive.org
- dbGaP (controlled access, application required): ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gap/
- GTEx portal v9: gtexportal.org
- Synapse (PsychENCODE / CommonMind / ROSMAP): synapse.org

### Items less certain about
1. **Li et al. 2026 Korean lung cell count**: preprint metadata states 37 cell types and "abundant" cells but does not give a precise total. Verify in supplement.
2. **Mereu et al. 2025 pancreas VASA-seq fraction**: total study has 4M+ cells across multiple modalities; VASA-seq fraction is unclear. Preprint deposit status pending.
3. **TenK10K accession EGAS50000001654** identified by search; verify against Garvan press materials.
4. **Hagemann-Jensen Smart-seq3 / Smart-seq3xpress accessions** (GSE149224, GSE176122) are best-guess from training memory; verify on GEO.
5. **HipSci long-read transcriptome**: web search returned no confirmed long-read HipSci deposit. iPSCORE and HipSci bulk RNA-seq exist; long-read does not appear deployed at population scale.
6. **GTEx v9 portal does not include new RNA-seq or QTL data** vs v8 — the dataset to use is GTEx v8 bulk plus Glinos et al. 2022 long-read complement.

### Access restrictions (likely require data access applications)
- GTEx (phs000424): dbGaP
- OneK1K (EGAS00001005634): EGA
- TenK10K Phase 1 (EGAS50000001654): EGA
- PsychENCODE / CommonMind / ROSMAP: Synapse, individual project-level approval
- TOPMed: dbGaP, individual study-level approval
- MAGE: Zenodo / dbGaP combination
- iPSCORE: dbGaP

### Bottom-line recommendation

If "full coverage × genotype" is the hard constraint and single-cell is *not*, the top dataset is the **GTEx v8 bulk + Glinos long-read combination** — ~948 donors × 54 tissues × paired WGS × full-coverage Illumina + Nanopore on 88 samples.

If single-cell **and** full coverage are both required, **Cuomo et al. 2020 (Smart-seq2, 125 donors, ~36K cells, ENA PRJEB14362) is the only large-scale option** — Jerber, despite its larger donor/cell count, is 10x Chromium and does not satisfy the full-coverage constraint.

The next-tier additions beyond Cuomo are:

1. **Li et al. 2026 Korean lung sc-isoQTL** (long-read, 129 donors) — only single-cell dataset that matches Cuomo on donor count and adds isoform-level supervision (Nanopore).
2. **MAGE bulk RNA-seq** (731 globally diverse donors) — explicit ancestry diversity at the bulk level.
3. **TenK10K Phase 1** (1,925 donors × 5M cells, 10x) — scale benchmark; not full coverage, listed for context.

**Net finding**: no full-length single-cell protocol other than Smart-seq2 has yet produced a >100-donor public cohort with paired WGS. Cuomo (Smart-seq2) and Li et al. 2026 (Nanopore long-read) are the only two single-cell datasets that simultaneously satisfy (full coverage) AND (>50 donors) AND (paired genotype).
